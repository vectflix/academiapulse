Biology: Transport in Animals (The Heart)

Full Form 2 Study Notes

1. Introduction: The heart is a myogenic muscular pump located in the thoracic cavity, protected by the pericardium. It is divided into four chambers: the right atrium, right ventricle, left atrium, and left ventricle.

[attachment_0](attachment)

2. The Cardiac Cycle: This is the sequence of events during one heartbeat. It consists of Atrial Systole (atria contract), Ventricular Systole (ventricles contract), and Diastole (the heart relaxes and fills with blood).

3. Heart Valves: The tricuspid and bicuspid (mitral) valves prevent backflow into the atria, while semi-lunar valves at the base of the aorta and pulmonary artery prevent backflow into the ventricles.

Chemistry: Electrochemistry

Advanced Form 4 Notes

1. Oxidation and Reduction (Redox): Oxidation is the loss of electrons or the addition of oxygen. Reduction is the gain of electrons or the removal of oxygen.

2. Electrochemical Cells: A voltaic cell converts chemical energy into electrical energy. It consists of two half-cells connected by a salt bridge, which maintains ionic neutrality.

3. Electrolysis: This is the decomposition of an electrolyte by an electric current. Common examples include the electrolysis of molten Lead (II) Bromide and concentrated Sodium Chloride (Brine).

Physics: Thin Lenses & Modern Physics

Detailed Physics Notes

1. Thin Lenses: Lenses are used to focus or diverge light. Convex lenses (converging) are thicker in the middle, while concave lenses (diverging) are thinner in the middle.

2. Photoelectric Effect: This is the emission of electrons from a metal surface when light of a sufficient frequency (threshold frequency) is shone on it.
