Biology: The Complete Study

Form 2: The Heart & Transport System

Detailed Anatomical Breakdown

The human heart is a myogenic pump located in the thoracic cavity. It is protected by the **Pericardium**, which secretes pericardial fluid to minimize friction during contraction. It consists of four distinct chambers: the right and left atria (receiving chambers) and the right and left ventricles (pumping chambers). The **Left Ventricle** is significantly thicker than the right (by about 3 times) because it must generate enough force to pump blood through the entire systemic circulation, whereas the right only pumps to the lungs.

The Cardiac Cycle

1. **Atrial Systole:** The SA Node sends an electrical impulse, causing both atria to contract. Blood is pushed through the Tricuspid and Bicuspid valves into the ventricles.
2. **Ventricular Systole:** The ventricles contract. The pressure closes the AV valves (the 'LUB' sound) and forces the Semi-lunar valves open. Blood enters the Aorta and Pulmonary Artery.
3. **Diastole:** The heart muscle relaxes. The Semi-lunar valves close (the 'DUB' sound) to prevent backflow into the heart.

Chemistry: Comprehensive Course

Form 4: Electrochemistry

Standard Electrode Potentials

Electrochemistry involves the study of electron transfer. We use the **Standard Hydrogen Electrode (SHE)** as a reference to determine the E° of other elements. A more negative E° value indicates a stronger reducing agent (easier to lose electrons). The **Salt Bridge** is crucial as it completes the circuit and maintains electrical neutrality by allowing ion migration.

Industrial Electrolysis: The Downs Cell

In the extraction of Sodium, molten NaCl is electrolyzed. Calcium Chloride is added to lower the melting point of NaCl from 800°C to 600°C to save energy. Chlorine gas is produced at the Anode, and Sodium metal is collected at the Cathode.
