Mathematics: Complete Compendium (Form 1 - 4)

1. Core Algebra & Number Systems

Detailed Notes:

The journey starts with Natural Numbers, Factors, and Prime Factorization. As we progress to Form 2 and 3, we master Quadratic Expressions and Equations. A quadratic equation is of the form ax² + bx + c = 0. We solve these using factorization, completing the square, or the quadratic formula. In Form 4, we explore Linear Programming, where we use inequalities to define regions on a Cartesian plane and optimize objective functions for business scenarios.

2. Calculus (Differentiation & Integration)

Detailed Notes:

Differentiation: This is the process of finding the derivative or gradient function. If y = xⁿ, then dy/dx = nxⁿ⁻¹. We use this to find the equations of tangents and normals to a curve. Integration: This is the reverse of differentiation. It is primarily used to find the Area under a Curve and the volume of solids of revolution. The integral of xⁿ is (xⁿ⁺¹)/(n+1) + C.

3. Geometry, Trigonometry & Matrices

We study the properties of circles (Chords and Tangents), 3D Geometry (Angles between lines and planes), and Trigonometry III, which involves solving trigonometric equations and graphing sine and cosine waves. Matrices are used to solve simultaneous equations and perform transformations like reflection, rotation, and enlargement in the Cartesian plane.

Revision Challenge:

  • 1. Differentiate y = 2x³ - 5x² + x - 10.
  • 2. Find the inverse of matrix M = [[2, 1], [4, 3]].
  • 3. Calculate the area of a triangle with sides 5cm, 7cm and an included angle of 60°.

Biology: The Complete Compendium (Form 1 - 4)

1. Classification, Cell Physiology & Nutrition

Detailed Notes:

Biology begins with Classification I & II, where we use the Binomial Nomenclature system. In Cell Physiology, we study Diffusion, Osmosis, and Active Transport. Osmosis is specifically the movement of water molecules from a dilute solution to a concentrated one through a semi-permeable membrane. This is vital for water absorption in plant roots. Nutrition in Plants covers Photosynthesis, occurring in the chloroplasts, while Nutrition in Animals details the human digestive system, from ingestion to egestion.

2. Transport in Plants and Animals (The Heart)

The Mammalian Heart:

The heart is a myogenic muscular organ. It consists of the Right/Left Atria and Right/Left Ventricles. The Left Ventricle is thicker because it pumps blood to the whole body. The Cardiac Cycle involves Atrial Systole, Ventricular Systole, and Diastole. Valves like the Tricuspid and Bicuspid prevent the backflow of blood, ensuring a one-way circulatory system.

3. Genetics, Evolution & Ecology

In Genetics, we study Mendelian inheritance, DNA replication, and mutations. Evolution covers theories like Lamarckism and Darwinism (Natural Selection). Finally, Ecology examines the interaction of living organisms with their environment, focusing on energy flow in food chains and webs.

Chemistry: Comprehensive Repository (Form 1 - 4)

1. Atomic Structure, Chemical Families & Bonding

Detailed Notes:

Chemistry starts with the Atomic Structure: protons, neutrons, and electrons. Elements are organized in the Periodic Table into Groups and Periods. In Chemical Bonding, we distinguish between Ionic bonds (transfer of electrons) and Covalent bonds (sharing of electrons). Understanding the properties of giant ionic, molecular, and atomic structures is key to predicting melting points and conductivity.

2. Electrochemistry (Form 4 Core)

Electrolysis and Redox:

Electrochemistry involves redox reactions. Electrolysis is the decomposition of an electrolyte by an electric current. For example, the Downs Cell is used for Sodium extraction. We also study Voltaic Cells, which use chemical energy to produce electricity. The Salt Bridge is essential for maintaining ionic balance in these cells.

3. Organic Chemistry & Radioactivity

Organic Chemistry I & II cover Alkanes, Alkenes, Alkynes, Alkanols, and Alkanoic Acids. We focus on IUPAC nomenclature and chemical tests like the decolourisation of bromine water to test for unsaturation. Radioactivity covers alpha, beta, and gamma radiation, including half-life calculations and industrial applications like carbon dating.

Revision Challenge:

  • 1. Draw the structure of 2,2-dimethylpropane.
  • 2. State two factors that affect the preferential discharge of ions during electrolysis.
  • 3. Calculate the EMF of a cell: Zn(s)/Zn²⁺(aq) // Cu²⁺(aq)/Cu(s).

Physics: Complete Compendium (Form 1 - 4)

1. Mechanics, Force & Pressure

Detailed Notes:

Physics starts with Measurement and Force. We study the effects of force such as stretching (Hooke's Law) and turning (Moments). Pressure is defined as force per unit area ($P = F/A$). In liquids, pressure increases with depth ($P = hρg$). We also cover Newton’s Laws of Motion, where the second law ($F = ma$) is fundamental for calculating the motion of bodies under constant force.

2. Waves, Sound & Optics

This section covers Rectilinear Propagation of Light, Reflection at plane and curved surfaces, and Refraction (Snell's Law). We study Waves—both transverse and longitudinal—and their properties like interference and diffraction. Sound is explored as a longitudinal wave requiring a medium, focusing on echoes and the speed of sound in different materials.

3. Electricity, Magnetism & Modern Physics

Modern Physics includes the Photoelectric Effect (emissions of electrons from metal surfaces), **X-rays**, and **Radioactivity**. In Electricity, we move from simple circuits in Form 1 to complex networks, Heating Effect of current, and Electromagnetic Induction (Generators and Transformers) in Form 4.

Revision Challenge:

  • 1. State the law of flotation.
  • 2. Define the term 'half-life' of a radioactive substance.
  • 3. Calculate the work done when a force of 50N moves a block through 5 meters.

Geography: The Virtual Atlas (Form 1 - 4)

1. Introduction to Geography & The Solar System

Detailed Notes:

Geography is divided into Physical Geography (natural environment) and Human Geography (human activities). We study the Solar System, the Earth’s rotation (causing day and night), and revolution (causing seasons). Map Work is a core skill, involving the use of grid references, direction, and bearing to interpret topographical maps.

2. Internal & External Land Forming Processes

We explore Minerals and Rocks, identifying igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic types. Internal Processes include Vulcanicity, Faulting, and Folding, which create mountains and rift valleys. External Processes involve Weathering, Mass Wasting, and the action of rivers, glaciers, and wind in shaping the landscape.

3. Human Geography: Agriculture, Industry & Population

This section focuses on Agriculture (crop and livestock farming), **Mining**, **Forestry**, and **Fishing**. We also analyze **Industry**, **Transport and Communication**, and **Population** dynamics, including factors affecting population growth and settlement patterns in Kenya and globally.

Revision Challenge:

  • 1. Differentiate between a rock and a mineral.
  • 2. State three conditions necessary for the formation of a coral reef.
  • 3. Explain how the ITCZ influences rainfall in Kenya.

History & Government: The Kenyan Archive (Form 1 - 4)

1. Introduction to History, Migration & Early Inhabitants

Detailed Notes:

History begins with the study of our origins. We analyze the Migration and Settlement of the three main linguistic groups in Kenya: the Bantu, Nilotes, and Cushites. We study their social, economic, and political organizations before the 19th century. Early inhabitants like the Khoisan and Gumba are also discussed, alongside the impact of Indian Ocean Trade on the Coast of East Africa.

2. Colonial Rule, Struggle for Independence & Nationalism

This core section covers the Scramble and Partition of Africa and the establishment of British colonial rule. We explore African responses, including Resistance (Nandi, Agiriama) and Collaboration (Maasai, Wanga). The rise of nationalism is studied through the Mau Mau movement and the constitutional path to independence in 1963. We also cover the World Wars and their impact on African political awareness.

3. Government of Kenya, Constitution & Devolution

We study the Branches of Government: The Executive, Legislature, and Judiciary. A major focus is placed on the 2010 Constitution and the concept of Devolution (County Governments). We also analyze the electoral process, the Bill of Rights, and the challenges facing the Kenyan government today.

Revision Challenge:

  • 1. Name three sources of information on History and Government.
  • 2. State two methods used by the British to establish colonial rule in Kenya.
  • 3. List four functions of the Clerk of the National Assembly.

C.R.E: Complete Virtual Bible Study (Form 1 - 4)

1. Introduction to CRE & The Old Testament

We begin with the Creation stories in Genesis and the call of Abraham. The syllabus follows the history of Israel: the Exodus, the Sinai Covenant, and the roles of Prophets such as Amos, Jeremiah, and Nehemiah. We focus on their teachings about social justice, the judgment of God, and the promise of a New Covenant.

2. The New Testament & The Life of Jesus

This section details the Life and Teachings of Jesus Christ, including his miracles, parables, and his journey to the cross. We study the growth of the early church through the Acts of the Apostles and the letters of Paul, focusing on the unity of believers and the fruit of the Holy Spirit.

3. Christian Ethics & Contemporary Issues

The final part of the CRE syllabus applies Christian values to modern life. Topics include Christianity and Law, leisure, work, and the environment. We also discuss contemporary challenges like substance abuse, HIV/AIDS, and the Christian approach to wealth and poverty.

English & Literature: Language Mastery (Form 1 - 4)

1. Grammar, Punctuation & Writing Skills

Grammar is the backbone of the English language. We study Parts of Speech, Tenses, and the active/passive voice. Writing skills are emphasized through functional writing (letters, reports, minutes) and imaginative compositions. We also cover Oral Skills, focusing on pronunciation, intonation, and public speaking techniques.

2. Literature: Poetry & Oral Literature

Oral Literature involves the study of riddles, proverbs, and folk narratives. We analyze their social functions and stylistic features. In Poetry, we learn to analyze tone, mood, and persona, and identify literary devices such as metaphors, similes, and personification.

3. Literature: The Set Books

Students engage with compulsory set texts (Play and Novel) and optional texts. We analyze themes (e.g., corruption, family conflict, change vs. tradition) and characterization. Understanding the plot structure and the author's message is crucial for passing the Literature paper.

Kiswahili: Lugha na Fasihi (Form 1 - 4)

1. Sarufi na Matumizi ya Lugha

Maelezo ya Kina:

Kiswahili kinajumuisha Sarufi (Grammar), ambapo tunajifunza Ngeli za nomino (A-WA, KI-VI, LI-YA n.k.), aina za maneno, na uakifishaji. Pia tunasisitiza Insha (Composition) za aina mbalimbali kama vile barua, kumbukumbu, na insha za wasifu. Ustadi wa kusikiliza na kuzungumza unakuzwa kupitia matumizi sahihi ya msamiati.

2. Fasihi Simulizi na Fasihi Andishi

Fasihi Simulizi inahusisha ngano, methali, vitendawili, na ushairi simulizi. Tunachunguza umuhimu wake katika jamii ya sasa. Katika Fasihi Andishi, tunasoma vitabu teule (Riwaya, Tamthilia, na Hadithi Fupi), tukichambua maudhui kama vile uongozi mbaya, elimu, na nafasi ya mwanamke.

Agriculture: Science of Farming (Form 1 - 4)

1. Soil Science & Crop Production

Detailed Notes:

Agriculture focuses on sustainable production. We study Soil Fertility (Macro and Micro-nutrients) and how to maintain it through manuring and fertilizer application. Crop Production covers everything from land preparation to harvesting and storage of crops like maize, coffee, and tea. Pest and disease control is also a major focus.

2. Livestock Production & Farm Machinery

We analyze the management of Livestock (cattle, sheep, goats, poultry, and bees). Topics include nutrition (feeds), health (disease control), and breeding. Farm machinery and structures, such as fences, stores, and greenhouses, are also discussed to improve farm efficiency.

Business Studies: Virtual Commerce (Form 1 - 4)

1. Entrepreneurship & Office Practice

This subject introduces students to the business world. We cover **Entrepreneurship**, types of business units (Sole Proprietorships to Public Limited Companies), and **Insurance**. Office practice details the roles of various personnel and the importance of efficient communication and filing systems.

2. Economics & Accounting

In the senior forms, we dive into **Economics** (Supply and Demand, Inflation, and International Trade) and **Accounting**. Students learn to record transactions in the Ledger and prepare Financial Statements, including the Cash Book, Trial Balance, and the Balance Sheet.

Computer Studies: Digital Mastery (Form 1 - 4)

1. Computer Systems & Application Software

We start with **Hardware and Software** components. Application areas include Word Processing, Spreadsheets (Excel), and Databases (Access). We study how data is processed and stored in a computer system.

2. Networking, Programming & Ethics

Advanced topics include **Data Communication and Networking**, where we explore the Internet and LAN/WAN setups. We also introduce **Programming** logic and high-level languages, alongside **Data Security** and the ethical use of computers in society.
